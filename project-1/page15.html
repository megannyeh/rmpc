MORE ABOUT RESCUING DOGS FROM TAIWAN

Taiwan is a small island in East Asia with 20 million people. Unlike most developed countries, Taiwan has a high population of unwanted street dogs. Due to cultural differences, dogs in Taiwan are often viewed as worthless creatures and are never given a chance to have a home.

Some street dogs are sent to local government shelters and are only given 12 days to live before they are put down. Some dogs are rescued and brought to no-kill shelters. Unfortunately, most of these no-kill shelters are independently run by a single owner and overcrowded with hundreds or even thousands of dogs. These shelters often do not have enough resources to take care of all of the dogs in need and as a result, many of these dogs are locked in cages their entire lives.

When considering getting a dog as a pet, I highly encourage you to adopt/rescue rather than buying one. There are many dogs in local government shelters that are in need of a home and would benefit from adoption.
